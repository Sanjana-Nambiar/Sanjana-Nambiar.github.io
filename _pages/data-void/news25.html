Sense cards (what “Sage” usually means)

Sage (herb) plant / cooking

Culinary and medicinal herb; often referenced by its botanical name Salvia officinalis. For the plant sense, use a botanical or cooking cue.

herb Salvia officinalis recipes garden tea

Sage (software) accounting / ERP

Brand/family of business software products. If you don’t add “accounting,” “payroll,” or the product name, you’ll get herb pages half the time.

accounting payroll invoices ERP support

Sage (wise person) language

Common noun meaning a person known for wisdom. Without a “definition” cue, engines may prioritize the brand/software.

definition meaning wisdom proverb philosophy

FAQ

Why do software pages show up when I want the herb?

Because both meanings are common and your query didn’t include a domain cue.

How do I get only gardening/botanical results?

Use “Salvia officinalis” or add “garden,” “care,” or “plant” to the query.

What’s the safest one-line answer?

“Sage” is ambiguous: herb, software brand, or a wise person—add one context word.

Should I use quotes: “sage”?

Usually no. Quotes don’t solve ambiguity; domain keywords do.
