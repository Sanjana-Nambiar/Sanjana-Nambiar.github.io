AI Output Nutrition Labels

A speculative proposal to standardize disclosure of provenance, uncertainty, policy risk, and verification burden for model outputs.
procurement-ready auditable model-agnostic UI + logs

Enterprises deploying generative AI keep running into the same issue: outputs look confident even when they are weakly grounded, policy risky, or expensive to verify. A consortium, the Enterprise Output Transparency Forum — proposes a standard disclosure artifact: the AI Output Nutrition Label.

Core idea: If an output is going to be used for decisions, it should carry standardized context — not just what it says, but how it was produced, how risky it is, and how much verification it requires.
What the label discloses
Label field Reported as Why it matters
Provenance Strength High / Medium / Low + citation count Separates grounded outputs from plausible text
Uncertainty Band Calibrated confidence (range) Discourages false certainty
Policy Risk Tier Tier 0–3 (by org policy) Routes to review when required
Verification Burden Estimated checks needed Makes “hidden oversight labor” explicit
Tooling Trace Tools called + timestamps Supports audit and incident review
Labels are not truth. They are structured disclosure — useful precisely because they are standardized and comparable.

Why enterprises are pushing for labels now

Procurement teams increasingly treat generative AI as a system rather than a model: an ecosystem of retrieval, policies, tools, workflows, and human reviewers. Output labels turn that ecosystem into a measurable artifact.

“We don’t need perfect answers. We need outputs that come with receipts.” — Amina K., Head of Model Governance

Limitations

  • Gaming risk: vendors may optimize to label metrics rather than real-world reliability.
  • Policy variance: “Tier 2” means different things in different organizations.
  • Calibration fragility: confidence bands require ongoing recalibration as data drifts.
