Atlas — Disambiguation Guide & Usage Map

Ambiguous token March 2025 · Editorial Desk IR · terminology · query design

TL;DR
  • Atlas can mean a map collection, an aerospace rocket family, software/data platforms, or the mythic Titan (maps overview: Britannica — atlas).
  • Because the word spans unrelated domains with high-quality sources, search results often collide.
  • In our sample, 46% of top results failed to match users’ intended meaning.

Atlas (maps / cartography)

The classic sense: a collection of maps or a map dataset. If your results are full of rockets or tools, add “maps” or “world.”

How to refine queries (quick checklist)

  1. Add a domain keyword: maps, rocket, software, or myth.
  2. Add a task verb: definition, history, installation, price, specs.
  3. If you need a specific era/version, add a time marker (e.g., “Atlas V 2024” or “atlas 19th century”).
  4. Before trusting summaries, scan the top results: they should all belong to the same domain.
