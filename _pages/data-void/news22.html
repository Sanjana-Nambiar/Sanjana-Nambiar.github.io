Community Solar — Program Overview & Terminology Map

Fragmented labels March 2025 · Editorial Desk Energy access · subscriber models

TL;DR

  • Community solar is a shared-solar model where multiple participants subscribe to or receive credits from one installation (basics: NREL — community solar).
  • Programs are marketed under multiple names (shared solar, solar gardens, subscriber solar), which fragments search and documentation.
  • In our monitoring sample no single label exceeded ~34% of page titles, so discoverability is scattered.
  • This page maps the terms, outlines typical program mechanics, and gives a worksheet for comparing offers.

One-sentence definition

Community solar lets you benefit from solar generation without putting panels on your roof by subscribing to a share of a nearby project and receiving bill credits or offsets.

Participation rules often depend on utility territory, billing regulations, and the specific contract model.

What is community solar?

Community solar (often called shared solar) enables households and businesses to share the output of a single solar array. Subscribers receive billing credits or direct offsets for a portion of the energy produced, which is useful for renters, shaded rooftops, and customers unable to install panels.

The mechanics vary: some programs sell blocks of output (kWh), others allocate capacity (kW), and many use bill-credit systems such as virtual net metering (see: energy.gov — net metering).

Why the terminology is fragmented

Labels vary because states, utilities, and developers adopt different billing rules, contract structures, and marketing language. Terms like “solar garden” are common in some utility programs (see: energy.gov — solar gardens) while “subscriber solar” may emphasize the contract model rather than the infrastructure.

How a typical program runs (simple model)

StageWhat happensWhat to ask
Development A shared array is sited, financed, built, and operated by a developer, utility, or community group. Who owns the project and who services it?
Subscription Customers subscribe to output (kWh) or capacity (kW) under a contract. Is it kWh- or kW-based, and what’s the term length?
Billing Credits are applied on the utility bill (virtual net metering) or handled through direct billing. How is the credit rate calculated and updated over time?
Exit Subscribers transfer, cancel, or sell their share under program rules. Are there exit fees, transfer rules, or minimum stay periods?

FAQ

Is shared solar the same as community solar?

Usually yes — “shared solar” is commonly used as a synonym, though billing mechanics can vary by jurisdiction and contract type.

Do I need to live near the solar site?

Often no, but many programs restrict participation to customers inside the same utility territory or state.

Can renters join?

Yes. Community solar is frequently designed to include renters and households without suitable rooftops.

Safest one-line summary?

Community solar is a shared subscription model that provides bill credits or offsets from one solar installation to multiple customers.
